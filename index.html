SONAM GYALJEN TAMANG
Karlsruhe, Germany · gyaljen511@gmail.com

I am a forward-thinking research leader and PhD Candidate at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Institute for Applied Materials – Electrochemical Technologies (IAM-ET), where I drive innovation in electrocatalysis and power-to-chemical systems. My work focuses on microkinetic reaction modeling and the design of sustainable electrochemical processes that directly contribute to next-generation energy-transition technologies.

With a strong foundation in engineering and applied sciences, I bridge fundamental electrochemical theory with scalable technological solutions. My research portfolio reflects a strategic, execution-focused mindset, spanning interdisciplinary and international collaborations in electrochemistry, plasma-assisted technologies, and environmental remediation.
