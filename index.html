SONAM GYALJEN TAMANG
Karlsruhe, Germany · gyaljen511@gmail.com

Sonam Gyaljen Tamang, M.Eng. is a forward-thinking research leader and PhD Candidate at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Institute for Applied Materials – Electrochemical Technologies (IAM-ET), where he drives innovation in electrocatalysis and power-to-chemical systems. His work centers on microkinetic reaction modeling and the design of sustainable electrochemical processes that directly contribute to next-generation energy-transition technologies.

With a strong foundation in engineering and applied sciences, Sonam bridges fundamental electrochemical theory with scalable technological solutions. His research portfolio reflects a strategic, execution-focused mindset, spanning interdisciplinary and international collaborations in electrochemistry, plasma-assisted technologies, and environmental remediation.

Known for his analytical precision, systems-level thinking, and calm leadership, Sonam operates with a CEO’s perspective on research—aligning scientific depth with long-term impact. He represents a new generation of scientist-leaders translating complex science into actionable innovation for a sustainable future.
